Full Title: Open Loops in Fiction

URL: https://kajsotala.fi/2021/03/open-loops-in-fiction/

Highlights from March 15th, 2021.

A fiction-writing trick I find particularly compelling are open loops.
Even as loops are closed, new ones get opened. As the author is doing this, various characters are established. Their goals and intentions set up long-term open loops: ones that require a whole chapter or the whole story to resolve.
A cliffhanger is an example: you want to know how the hero survives, so your thoughts keep looping back to the situation, trying to figure out what happens next. But you need the author to tell you.
The first sentence of the story is meaningful on its own, but also makes reference to something else; that something else is required to understand the full meaning. That sets up an open loop; I want to know more.
